# Code of Conduct

OpenKoASR aims to be a practical, reproducible benchmark for Korean ASR. Contributors are expected to keep discussions technical, respectful, and focused on improving the project.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful of different backgrounds, institutions, and levels of experience.
- Critique code, methodology, and evidence rather than people.
- Make result claims with enough detail to reproduce them.
- Follow dataset licenses and privacy restrictions.
- Avoid posting raw private data, credentials, or restricted artifacts.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language
- Knowingly submitting misleading benchmark results
- Publishing restricted data, private transcripts, credentials, or personal information
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions after maintainers ask to refocus

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and close issues or pull requests that violate this code. Severe or repeated violations may lead to repository bans.
